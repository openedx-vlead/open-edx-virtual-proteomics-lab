A. In-Gel Digestion

Materials Required

Chemicals:

  1. Ammonium bicarbonate (NH4HCO3) [MW: 79.06]
  2. Dithiothreitol (DTT) [MW: 154.24]
  3. Iodoacetoamide (IAA) [MW: 185]
  4. Trypsine (Proteomics/ sequencing grade)
  5. Acetonitrile (ACN)
  6. Trifluro Acetic Acid (TFA)

Solutions:

    1. Stain removal solution: 1:1 (v/v) ACN: 100mM NH4HCO3. Dissolve 79.06 mg Ammonium bicarbonate (NH4HCO3) in 10mL MilliQ water and add 1mL of ACN to 1mL of this solution.
    1. Dehydration solution: 2:1 mixture of ACN and 50mM ammonium bicarbonate buffer.
    1. Reduction solution: 10mM DTT in 100mM ammonium bicarbonate buffer. Dissolve 7.7mg DTT in 5mL of 100mM ammonium bicarbonate buffer.
    1. Alkylation solution: 55mM IAA in 100mM ammonium bicarbonate buffer. Dissolve 10.16mg IAA in 1mL of 100mM ammonium bicarbonate buffer.
    1. Trypsin solution: Dissolve 20µg lyophilized trypsin powder in 100µL of 1mM HCl solution. Mix properly and add 900µL of 40mM ammonium bicarbonate solution made in 9% ACN. Store at -20OC freezer. The concentration of this stock solution is 20µg /mL. Store this stock solution in different aliquots to avoid multiple freeze thaw cycles.
  1. Extraction solution: 0.1% TFA in 60% ACN solution.

Procedure

    1. Rinse the entire gel with water for few hours with intermittent changing of water.
    1. Excise protein spots/bands with a clean sterile scalpel and place the gel slice into a 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube.
    1. Cut the slices into small cubes (1 x 1 mm) while avoiding too small pieces as those can clog pipette tips.
    1. Add 50μL of stain removal solution (for large gel slices take enough liquid to cover it completely) and rotate on a shaker for 30 min at room temperature for removal of the stain from the gel pieces. Change the solution after every 10 min.
    1. Add 50μL dehydration solution and incubate at room temperature, until the gel pieces become white and stick together. Change the solution after every 10 min. Spin the gel pieces down (at ~2000g for 30 sec) and remove all liquid.
    1. Add 30–50μL of the reduction solution to completely cover the gel pieces. Incubate for 30 min at 56oC. Chill down the tubes to room temperature; add 50μL of dehydration solution, incubate for 10 min and remove the liquid.
    1. Add 30–50 μL (more for a larger gel slices) of the alkylation solution and incubate for 20 min in dark at room temperature, add 50μL of dehydration solution, incubate for 10 min and remove the liquid.
    1. Add 25 μL trypsin solution (~500ng) to the dry gel pieces and keep on ice for absorption of the enzyme by the gel pieces.
    1. Add 25µL ammonium bicarbonate buffer (in which trypsin is prepared) and incubate at 37oC for overnight (12-16 hours). Next morning, stop the reaction by keeping the reaction mixtures in ice.
    1. Add 50μL of extraction solution and vortex for 10 min at room temperature. Collect supernatant in a fresh microcentrifuge tube. Repeat the extraction step thrice and pool the collected supernatant.
  1. Make small aliquots of the extracted solution and store at -20OC freezer

B. In-Solution Digestion

Materials Required

Chemicals:

  1. Ammonium bicarbonate (NH4HCO3) [MW: 79.06]
  2. Dithiothreitol (DTT) [MW: 154.24]
  3. Iodoacetoamide (IAA) [MW: 185]
  4. Trypsine
  5. Acetonitrile (ACN)
  6. Formic acid (FA)

Solution Preparation:

  1. Trypsin stock: Dissolve 20µg lyophilized trypsin powder in 100µL of 1mM HCl solution. Mix properly; add 900µL 40mM ammonium bicarbonate solution made in 9% ACN. Store at -20OC freezer. The concentration of this stock solution is 20µg /mL i.e. 20ng/µL
  2. Digestion buffer: Weigh 10 mg of the ammonium bicarbonate and dissolve in 2.5mL of ultrapure water for a final concentration of ~50mM. This solution can be stored at 4°C for up to two months
  3. Reducing buffer: Weigh 8 mg of DDT and dissolve in 500μL of ultrapure water to make a final concentration of ~100mM. Store the reducing buffer at -20°C.
  4. Alkylation buffer: Prepare alkylation buffer just before use. Weigh 9 mg of iodoaceamide (IAA) and keep it in an aluminum foil-wrapped tube to avoid exposure to light. Add 500µL of ultrapure water to make a final concentration of ~100mM.
  5. Extraction buffer: Mix 100µL formic acid and 200µL acetonitrile in 10mL of distilled water.

Note: Protein Concentrations: The procedure described below has been found to work well with protein concentrations in the range of 0.1 – 1.0 mg/mL. This concentration range will allow working with 1-10μg of protein in 10μL total volume for the digestion reaction.

Procedure

  1. Add 15µL of digestion buffer and 1.5µL of reducing buffer to a microcentrifuge tube.
  2. Add 10μl (10μg) of the protein solution to the tube and adjust the final volume with MilliQ water.
  3. Incubate the sample at 95°C for 5 min and allow cooling at room temperature.
  4. Add 3µL of alkylation buffer to the tube and incubate in the dark at room temperature for 20 min.
  5. Add 25µL (500ng) of trypsin stock solution to the reaction tube and incubate at 37°C for overnight.
  6. Add 30-40µL of extraction buffer and incubate for 30min at room temperature in a shaking condition.
  7. Add 1% of glacial acetic acid (v/v) to stop the reaction.
  8. Make small aliquots of the sample and store at -20°C for further use. Digested peptides are further processed using Zip-Tip C 18 pipette tips prior to the MS analysis.
